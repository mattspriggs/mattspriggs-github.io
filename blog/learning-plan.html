Bootcamp Learning Plan

The Destination

As a serial entrepreneur, I hope to eventually be able to either be a part of a start-up or launch my own full stack web development firm. I also have an interest in doing game development but feel that full stack development is a better path for me to start from. It may also make sense to work for someone while I adapt to being in a new career field and new to the New Zealand culture.

I am concerned that as a professional with a long history and as a new migrant to New Zealand that my age, experience and not having New Zealand work history, may work against me and could push me into work outside of tech (again).

I will need to keep tabs on what I can expect in the job market and/or as an entrepreneur as the bootcamp comes to an end. Thankfully there will be 2 weeks at the end to focus on this exact thing.

Strengths & Limitations

  • Strengths
    • My strenghts continue to be what I believed them to be during foundations:
      • I have strong self-discipline and have demonstrated it many times and in many ways in the past.
      • I have years of work and academic study in remote, hybrid and onsite locations. This means that I can and enjoy working in all of those environments. I work well both independently and collaboratively
      • I really enjoy problem solving and coding certainly provides many opportunities to problem solve!
      • I am a very creative person and enjoy seeing ideas come to life. This will provide me with continual drive throughout the bootcamp.
      • I am a life long learner, having more formal education than is probably advisable. I really enjoy learning. Having a focus on continual learning has required me to adopt a growth mindset. This is not always easy to maintain, but I have had success in the past and believe I will be able to continue to do this successfully during the bootcamp.
  • Limitations
    • Similar to my strengths, I anticipate that my limitations will continue to be very much the same as they were during Foundations:
      • My perfectionism can definitely get in the way. I tend to work on things too long with only minimal improvements, when I could have simply let it be "good enough".
      • My perfectionism can also drive me to overwork. This includes not taking breaks when I should and working too long.
      • Perhaps my biggest potential issue is not asking for help in a timely fashion. I tend to want to figure out solutions on my own, even when I can't see my way to the end after spending significant time working on it. I will need to be very cognizant of this during the bootcamp.

Soft Skill Development

The most important soft skill for me to develop during the bootcamp, is a better understanding of New Zealand culture and work place norms and how I can fit into it without trying to change who I am. As a new migrant, this will be critical for me to successfully assimilate into my new country and home.

Expectations

  • Expectations of the Bootcamp Team
    • I have the expectation that the bootcamp team will ber available to provide guidance and to provide an intensive but supportive learning experience.
    • I do not, and prefer not, to have them solve my problems for me unless I clearly have indicated that I need them to.
  • Self Expectations
    • I expect that I will set and keep to schedule, take regular breaks away from the computer and make sure that I get enough sleep.
    • I expect myself to listen and understand others before seeking to be understood.
    • I expect that I will work collaboratively whenever it is possible. I find that I learn a lot from working with others and if I find an opportunity to teach someone something, it further solidifies my own understanding of that thing.

Learning Strengths & Limitations

I have really strong self discipline; can work independently or collaboratively; enjoy problem solving and creating; have a continuos learning and growth mindset.

LIMITATIONS:
I can be a perfectionist and overwork. Another cosistent issue is not taking breaks when I should. I am also TERRIBLE at asking for help.

Soft Skills

I want to be kinder to myself, focus on delivering quality but not perfect products and develop an understanding of New Zealand's work environment and ethic.

Workload Commitment

  • I commit to:
    • Start work by 8 AM and end no later than 8 PM unless I am in crunch mode.
    • For every 4 hours I work I will take a minimum of a 15 minute break AWAY FROM THE KEYBOARD.
    • To deal with stress and potential conflicts, I will not react in the moment but instead will step back, listen and try to understand before responding.
      • This may require me to
        • Take a walk
        • Do some Tai Chi
        • Go for coffee
        • Anything completely different to let my emotional response clear

Help Commitment

I commit to asking for help from my fellow cohort members after I am unable to fix an issue myself within 30 minutes. If that fails I will seek assistance from Dev Academy facilitators

Expectations

I expect the facilitators to guide me to resources to help me solve my own problem. I have no expectation and would prefer that they not just hand me solution unless I am at my wits end.

Scheduling

I will spend my mornings doing my morning routine of exercise, etcetera and that is non-negotiable for me. The only other commitments I may have will be around appointments. There are no other regular commitments that I have not cleared.
