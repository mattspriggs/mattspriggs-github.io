Neuroplasticity & Growth Mindset

The Beneifit of Understanding Neroplasticity

The guru of the concept of neuroplasticity, Dr. Carol Dwek, has studied and provided an important concept for human growth and potential - the brain is NOT fixed at birth but is instead maleable and adapts and changes through out a person's life

Understanding that the brain is not a static, hard set part of the body opens up the possibility for anyone to improve at things, even just by imagining and mentally performing that thing. This means that anyone can engage with learning a skill when they understand that engaging in that thing will have benefits and improvement.

Dr. Dwek's research has shown that while physically engaging in that activity will have greater improvement, it is possible to practice it mentally when not engaged in the activity and still improve that skill. Mentally engaging in the skill will accelerate your improvement when you do engage in that activity physically again later. The gains from physically engaging in the activity post mental engagement are greater than without that period of mental engagement.

Engaging Neuroplasticity

  • From an article from Accelerate Learning Community of the University of Utah, there are 5 key components to engaging your in neuroplasticity:
    • Challenge & Novelty: it should be a skill that is new to you and not too easy to learn. This is essential for creating change in your brain
    • Intention: The skill must have some kind of meaning, relevance or importance to you. The idea of gaining something of value from engaging in this new skill will keep you engaged when it becomes difficult
    • Specific Attention: Skills that are practiced tend to improve and the more a person focusses on the tasks the more likely change will occur.
    • Repetition & Intensity: Short bouts of intense repetition are needed to create new neural connections. Doing something once is not likely to be sufficient.
    • Time: Neuroplastic change takes time for real structural and functional change to occur in the brain and the body. It is also normal to hit periods of plateaus while the change is being shaped. It is best to keep at it to overcome a plateau.

Growth Mindset

A Growth Mindset is the belief that people are good at things where they have built that ability, whereas those who are not good at it have not put in the work. In short you are in control of your abilities and your abilities are not fixed. The growth mindset is based with the understaing of neuroplasticity and the focus on the ability to build skills by re putting in deliberate effort. A growth mindset is essential for continually improving.

Keys to a Growth Mindset

  • (Highlights from Trevor Ragan) With a growth mindset:
    • Belief - One believes that skills are built and you can learn and grow in a deliberate way
    • Focus - The focus is on the process of getting better, not on proving you are competent
    • Key ingredients for growth:
      • Effort - Is useful and leads to growth
      • Challenges - Embrace and perservere through challenges and frame the challenge as an opportunity
      • Mistakes - Use them to learn from and don't be afraid of them
      • Feedback - Appreciate and use feedback
